    • La Trinidad:

    A Taste of the Highlands

    Explore strawberry farms, vegetable stalls, local treats, and mountain-made food experiences in La Trinidad, Benguet.

    Taste the Highlands. Live the Story.

    La Trinidad is more than just a market; it's a community painted on a mountainside. Our tours are not just about eating; they are about walking through strawberry fields with the farmers who tend them, learning the rhythm of the vegetable trading post, and hearing the stories behind every dish. We invite you to pull up a chair at the family table.

    Meet our Guides

    Juan Baclay

    Juan has been tilling the soil of La Trinidad for over 40 years. He doesn’t just farm strawberries; he talks to them. As your guide through the red fields, he’ll show you the difference between a sweet strawberry and a sour one, teach you how to properly pinch the stem, and share the hard truth about climate change and how it affects the harvest. He is humble, soft-spoken, and possesses a deep, ancestral knowledge of the land that you can’t find in any book.

    Jules Manipon

    Jules has been a fixture at the La Trinidad Vegetable Trading Post since he was a boy helping his father haul sacks of carrots. Now, he navigates the chaotic, colorful aisles like a symphony conductor. He knows every farmer by name, every trader's reputation, and the best stalls for heirloom vegetables you won’t find in Manila. With Jules, the 4 AM market rush transforms from overwhelming to exhilarating, as he deciphers the bidding wars and shares the stories behind the produce that feeds the nation.

    Maria Fianza

    A proud daughter of Benguet, Iya represents the new generation bridging the gap between tradition and tourism. Having grown up helping her parents sell produce at the famous La Trinidad Vegetable Trading Post, she knows every vendor, every shortcut, and the best time to snag the freshest sayote. She is energetic, fluent in both English and the local dialect, and loves explaining the chaotic, beautiful symphony of the 3 AM market. She doesn't just show you around; she introduces you to her kailyan townmates, making you a part of the community for a day.

    What to Taste Here

    From fresh of strawberries to warm strawberry taho, homemade jams, cakes, and more. La Trinidad turns the humble strawberry into a feast that taste the highlands in every sweet bite.
